Popular Artwork at the Met

  • Landscape--Scene from "Thanatopsis" by Asher Brown Durand

    Inspired by William Cullen Bryant's poem "Thanatopsis," this landscape was first exhibited at the National Academy of Design in 1850.

  • Bridge over a Pond of Water Lilies by Claude Monet

    In 1893, Monet purchased land with a pond to build something "for the pleasure of the eye." The result was this water-lily garden.

  • Wheat Field with Cypresses by Vincent Van Gogh

    Van Gogh regarded this work as one of his “best” summer landscapes and made two versions, one of which was a gift for his mother and sister.
